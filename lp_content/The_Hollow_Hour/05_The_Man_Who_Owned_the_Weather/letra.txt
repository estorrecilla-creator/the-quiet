He measured rain before it fell,
and billed the sky for every drop.
Called it foresight, called it service,
never once was told to stop.

He owned the clouds by Tuesday,
had the thunder under lease.
Told the farmers it was progress.
Told himself it was for peace.

We called it foresight. It was theft
dressed patiently, and it did not stop.

The man who owned the weather
sends the bill for every storm.
He never lies. He just names things
after himself, and calls it form.

Now he owns the drought as well —
sells the rain back, drop by drop.
We signed for it. We always do.
That's the part that doesn't stop.
