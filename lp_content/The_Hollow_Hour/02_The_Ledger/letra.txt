I keep a ledger by the bed,
one line for every hour spent.
Nobody told me to start it.
Nobody told me when it ends.

The ledger doesn't ask for reasons,
it just wants the number down.
I've filled four books since I stopped feeling
anything worth writing about.

I don't confess. I just record.
That's not the same as calm.

Column one, the hour.
Column two, the cost.
Nothing in between them —
just the ledger, and what's lost.

Someone asked to see the ledger.
I said there's nothing here to read.
Just hours, kept in order,
by a man who kept no other need.
