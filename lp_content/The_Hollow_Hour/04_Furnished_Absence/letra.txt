Furniture arranged like care,
a house rehearsing being home.
I answer to my name in rooms
that no one calls their own.

The chairs still face each other.
Nobody sits there anymore.
I dust the shape of company
that isn't coming through the door.

I keep the rooms the way they were.
Not for anyone. Just because.

Furnished absence, room by room.
Nothing missing. Nothing there.
I keep the house the way it was
for no one, everywhere.

Someone asked if I live alone.
I said the house doesn't think so.
It keeps setting places at the table
for people it used to know.

Furnished absence, room by room.
Nothing missing. Nothing there.
I keep the house the way it was
for no one, everywhere.
