I left the porch light on for years,
for someone who forgot the door.
The bulb's outlasted every reason
I first put it there for.

The neighbors think I'm waiting still.
I've stopped calling it that.
It's just a light now, doing its one job —
some habits outlive the fact.

I don't check the road anymore.
I just don't reach for the switch.

One light, one door,
one habit I never unlearned.
It isn't hope anymore —
it's just a light that stayed on while I turned.

The song still knows the way back home.
My legs don't, anymore.
But the light does what a light does —
it doesn't ask what for.

One light, one door,
one habit I never unlearned.
It isn't hope anymore —
it's just a light that stayed on while I turned.
